Frequently Asked Questions

What is FWD?

  • FWD is an independent implementation of the Progress 4GL, written in Java, and using a modern tech stack.
  • It is not affiliated with Progress Software Corporation; it is open source software which performs 100% automated, source-to-source conversion of a Progress ABL/4GL application into a compatible, drop-in replacement, written in Java.
  • It is flexible: future development can continue in the 4GL, be shifted to Java, or can be a mixture of both 4GL and Java development.
  • FWD allows you to migrate away from OpenEdge, without a rewrite, to a modern tech stack, eliminating all future Progress fees.

Why isn't the FWD booth in the Exhibit Hall this Year?

The PUG Challenge organizers refused FWD as an event sponsor, despite the gold level sponsorships we provided in years past. By offering a valuable alternative for organizations who are questioning the long term viability of OpenEdge in their tech stack, FWD is a technology which competes directly with OpenEdge. This does not sit well with conference organizers. In spite of the potential value of FWD to conference attendees and the strong interest shown in its sessions in the past, the FWD team was rejected as a PUG Challenge sponsor, exhibitor, and speaker. So, we arranged for alternate accommodations in the Hotel's Atrium.

If you have an application investment in the Progress ABL/4GL, you deserve to understand your options. Please stop by our booth, or reach out to us at fwd@goldencode.com to set up some time to discuss how you can get the most out of that investment over the long term.

What has been happening with FWD since the last PUG Challenge FWD sponsored?

A LOT!! We've been hard at work: rounding out the support for many legacy features (e.g., OO, data sets, unit testing, many more functions/methods/attributes); adding innovative features (e.g., language extensions, a spreadsheet widget, direct Java access); implementing new RDBMS support (e.g., MariaDB); improving performance; ...
